## How Cameroon's health system is structured

Cameroon's public health system is organised as a pyramid, with each level referring more complex cases upward:

- **Central / national level** — the largest hospitals, including general and central hospitals and university teaching hospitals, offering the broadest range of specialities and serving as national referral centres.
- **Regional level** — regional hospitals serving each of the country's ten regions, handling cases referred from districts.
- **District level** — district hospitals providing the bulk of secondary care close to communities.
- **Primary level** — integrated health centres (*centres de santé intégrés*) and sub-divisional health units that are most patients' first point of contact.

Alongside the public network sits a substantial **faith-based and private sector** — including long-established mission hospitals run by bodies such as the Cameroon Baptist Convention and Catholic and Protestant health networks — which delivers a significant share of care, especially in rural areas.

## Major referral hospitals in Yaoundé

As the political capital, Yaoundé hosts several of the country's flagship facilities:

- **Yaoundé Central Hospital (Hôpital Central de Yaoundé)** — one of Cameroon's largest public hospitals and a primary national referral centre, with a broad range of medical and surgical specialities and emergency services.
- **Yaoundé General Hospital (Hôpital Général de Yaoundé)** — a major referral hospital known for specialist and surgical care.
- **Yaoundé Gynaeco-Obstetric and Paediatric Hospital (HGOPY)** — focused on women's and children's health, including specialised maternal, neonatal and paediatric services.
- **University Teaching Hospital of Yaoundé (CHU)** — a teaching hospital combining patient care, training and research across multiple specialities.

## Major referral hospitals in Douala

Douala, the economic capital, anchors care for the coastal and western regions:

- **Douala General Hospital (Hôpital Général de Douala)** — the largest public hospital in the Littoral Region and a major referral centre for western and coastal Cameroon.
- **Laquintinie Hospital (Hôpital Laquintinie de Douala)** — a high-volume public hospital and key referral point, long central to healthcare in the city.

## Regional and faith-based hospitals

Beyond the two main cities, **regional hospitals** — such as those in Buea, Bamenda, Bafoussam, Garoua and other regional capitals — provide essential specialist care closer to where people live. **Mission and faith-based hospitals**, several of them with strong reputations built over decades, remain critical providers, particularly in rural and underserved areas.

## The common thread: every tier is digitising

What unites this landscape — from a national referral hospital to a district facility or a rural mission hospital — is the shared shift away from paper. Each tier faces the same operational pressures: long patient queues, fragmented records, revenue leakage, pharmacy stockouts, and the growing demands of Universal Health Coverage (CSU) claims and national reporting.

The facilities making the most progress are those adopting a **hospital management system** that fits their level:

- **Referral hospitals** need to coordinate many departments, specialities and high patient volumes from a single platform, often across multiple sites.
- **Regional and district hospitals** need core clinical, pharmacy and billing functions that work reliably even with intermittent connectivity.
- **Mission and rural facilities** need affordable, offline-tolerant tools that bring order to records and revenue without requiring a large IT team.

The destination is the same at every level: one connected record per patient, accurate billing, controlled pharmacy stock, and data that flows into national systems — the foundations of quality, accountable care.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What are the major referral hospitals in Yaoundé?
Yaoundé's flagship public facilities include Yaoundé Central Hospital (a primary national referral centre), Yaoundé General Hospital, the Yaoundé Gynaeco-Obstetric and Paediatric Hospital (HGOPY), and the University Teaching Hospital of Yaoundé.

### What are the main hospitals in Douala?
Douala's main public referral hospitals are Douala General Hospital — the largest in the Littoral Region — and Laquintinie Hospital, a high-volume facility long central to healthcare in the city.

### How is Cameroon's health system structured?
As a pyramid: central and national hospitals (including general, central and university teaching hospitals) at the top, then regional hospitals for each region, district hospitals for secondary care, and integrated health centres (*centres de santé intégrés*) at the primary level. A large faith-based and private sector operates alongside it.

### Are mission hospitals important in Cameroon?
Yes. Faith-based and mission hospitals — including long-established networks — deliver a significant share of care, especially in rural and underserved areas, and are critical providers within the overall system.
