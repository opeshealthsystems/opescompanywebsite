Overview

Built for African healthcare,
not adapted for it

OPES EMR is a fully bilingual (English/French) electronic medical records system designed specifically for hospitals, clinics, and health centres operating in Cameroon, the CEMAC region, and across Africa. Unlike imported systems retrofitted for local use, OPES EMR was designed from the ground up around how African healthcare facilities actually operate.

It captures the complete patient journey — from first contact through diagnosis, treatment, prescriptions, laboratory and radiology orders, billing, and long-term follow-up — all within a single connected record that travels with the patient across any OPES-connected facility.

Integrated natively with OPESCare Health ID, every patient has a unique lifelong health identity, and every clinician can access a complete medical history — not just what happened at this facility.

Who is OPES EMR for?

  • District hospitals and general clinics (10–500 beds)
  • Private polyclinics and specialist centres
  • Community health centres running government UHC programmes
  • NGO-operated facilities seeking interoperable reporting
  • Hospital networks wanting one unified patient record across branches

Patient Workflow

How OPES EMR moves a patient through your facility

From the moment a patient arrives to discharge, every step is captured — and every department sees the full picture in real time.

Step 1–2 · Arrival & Triage

Walk-in patients are registered in under 2 minutes. Returning patients are found by name, phone, or OPESCare Health ID. Nurses record vitals and chief complaint; the system assigns queue priority automatically. The doctor's queue updates in real time.

Step 3 · Clinical Consultation

The doctor sees the patient's complete history before the consultation begins — previous visits, chronic conditions, allergies, and current medications. Structured SOAP templates by specialty speed up documentation without losing clinical depth.

Step 4 · Orders

Lab requests appear instantly in the OPES Lab queue. Imaging requests go to RADIS. Prescriptions are sent electronically to PHARMIS for preparation. Drug-interaction alerts fire before any order is confirmed. Every ordered act logs automatically to billing.

Step 5–7 · Results, Billing & Discharge

Validated lab and radiology results flow back to the patient record automatically — no manual entry, no lost printouts. The billing clerk sees a pre-populated invoice from all acts. On discharge, a clinical summary is printed and the complete visit is synced to OPESCare Health ID.
