What content goes below the hero on the homepage?

After the hero and product grid, what should visitors see as they scroll? These sections build trust, drive partnerships, and convert visitors into leads.

✓

Ministry Alignment Banner

"Aligned with Cameroon Ministry of Health Digital Health Strategy 2026–2030." Government credibility stamp — critical for hospital procurement decisions and public tenders.

✓

Why OPES? — 3 Value Pillars

Bilingual · Built for Africa · Fully Interoperable. Short, scannable section. Answers the "so what?" question before the visitor leaves.

✓

The OPES Ecosystem Diagram

Visual showing how OPESCare (Health ID) sits at the center, with all 22 systems connected around it. Powerful for investors, ministry officials, and hospital groups who need to see the full picture.

✓

Featured Products Showcase

3–4 highlighted products (EMR, OPESCare, Hospital HIS, Triage) with screenshots or UI previews. Converts browsers who didn't click through the product grid.

✓

Opes Triage — Standalone Callout

Dedicated section for Opes Triage since it's your unique standalone sell ("Works even if you already have software"). Separate CTA: "Reduce Wait Times Today."

✓

Impact / Stats Section

Numbers: 20+ systems, X hospitals using OPES, CEMAC reach, bilingual, etc. Social proof even before you have big client logos. Animates on scroll for visual interest.

✓

Demo Request CTA Section

Full-width "Book a Free Demo" banner with a short form (name, facility type, email). This is your primary lead-capture point. Should appear at least once mid-page and once at the footer.

✓

Partnerships Section

"Partner with Us" block targeting hospitals, pharma companies, NGOs, investors, and insurance companies. Separate track from "buy our software" — important for your Articles' partnership model.

✓

Blog / Content Hub Preview

Latest 3 articles: product deep-dives, African digital health news, MoH policy updates. Critical for SEO/AEO/GEO. Google finds you through your content, not your homepage.

✓

Testimonials / Early Adopters

Quotes from hospitals, doctors, or pilot users. If you don't have them yet, reserve the slot and add them later. Even one strong quote dramatically lifts trust.
